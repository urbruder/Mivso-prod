# Long-Term Seller Nurture — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## 12-Month Seller Topic Ladder
1. “What changed in the local market?”
2. Equity check-in invitation.
3. Home prep timeline.
4. Pricing myths.
5. Low-cost presentation improvements.
6. Net-proceeds conversation.
7. Inspection and repair planning.
8. Timing the market without guessing.
9. Buyer feedback trends.
10. Decluttering and contractor planning.
11. Pre-listing strategy meeting invitation.
12. Launch readiness checklist.

## Seller Signals
- Asks about value.
- Mentions repairs, timing, relocation, family change, or job change.
- Requests contractor or staging advice.
- Clicks pricing or prep content repeatedly.
- Asks about taxes, net proceeds, or buying after selling.

## CTA Examples
“Would a fresh equity estimate be useful?” “Want a 10-minute prep timeline?” “Should I send the checklist I use before listing photos?”


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
