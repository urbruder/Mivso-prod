# Long-Cycle Buyer Nurture — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Buyer Readiness Ladder
| Stage | Focus | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| 18 months out | Dreaming and education | “Want a simple buying roadmap?” |
| 12 months out | Budget and criteria | “Should we outline your must-haves?” |
| 9 months out | Financing prep | “Want lender questions to ask?” |
| 6 months out | Search setup | “Should I set up a smart search?” |
| 3 months out | Pre-approval and tours | “Ready for a buyer consult?” |
| 0–2 months out | Active search | “Let’s review offer strategy.” |

## Monthly Topic Bank
Financing basics, down payment planning, closing costs, search criteria, offer terms, inspection expectations, appraisal basics, local market updates, timeline planning, home tour etiquette, buyer representation, and move logistics.

## Readiness Signals
Mentions lease end, asks about payments, saves homes, requests tours, obtains pre-approval, narrows criteria, asks about offers, or responds quickly to market updates.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
