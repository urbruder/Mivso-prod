# Expired Listing Re-Engager — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Failure-Diagnosis Grid
| Signal | Possible Meaning | Seller-Friendly Language |
|---|---|---|
| High showings, no offers | Price or buyer perception mismatch | Buyers were interested enough to visit but did not see enough value to act. |
| Low showings | Exposure, price, access, or first impression issue | The listing may not have created enough urgency in the first week. |
| Feedback mentions condition | Prep, staging, repair, or presentation gap | Small presentation changes may change how buyers compare the home. |
| Long days on market | Stale listing perception | A reset needs a new reason for buyers to look again. |
| Multiple reductions | Pricing story was unclear | The new plan should launch with a confident price position, not a reactionary one. |

## 10-Day Sequence Pattern
1. Day 1: Soft text or email acknowledging the listing status and offering a useful review.
2. Day 2: Phone call with curiosity-based opener.
3. Day 3: Send one-page relaunch idea tied to the home.
4. Day 5: Share one market fact or buyer-behavior insight.
5. Day 7: Invite a 15-minute strategy review.
6. Day 10: Final low-pressure check-in with a clear close-the-loop option.

## Message Style Rules
- Lead with empathy and usefulness.
- Make the seller feel understood, not corrected.
- Use phrases like “fresh launch,” “buyer perception,” “first-week urgency,” and “repositioning plan.”
- Avoid phrases like “your agent failed,” “you overpriced,” “I can guarantee,” or “I have buyers waiting” unless the agent has specific written proof.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
