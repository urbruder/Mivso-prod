# Listing Renewal / Re-List Pitch — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Relaunch Storyline
1. Acknowledge the seller’s goal and the effort already invested.
2. Review what the market actually told us.
3. Identify the few things that likely limited momentum.
4. Show what will be different this time.
5. Give the seller a clear choice and a recommended path.

## Relaunch Change Categories
| Category | Possible Change |
|---|---|
| Pricing | New launch price, pricing band, or offer-positioning strategy. |
| Presentation | Photos, staging, repairs, copy, floor plan, or video. |
| Access | Showing windows, open houses, lockbox process, feedback speed. |
| Marketing | Coming-soon, broker open, neighbor campaign, paid social, email blast. |
| Communication | Weekly report, showing feedback summary, decision checkpoints. |
| Urgency | First-week launch calendar, deadline strategy, fresh buyer reason to act. |

## Tone Rules
Use accountable language: “Here is what we learned,” “Here is what I would change,” and “Here is the path I recommend.” Avoid defensive language, excuses, and promises.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
