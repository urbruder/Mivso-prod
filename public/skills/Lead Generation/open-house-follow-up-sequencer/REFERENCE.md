# Open House Follow-Up Sequencer — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Visitor Segments
| Segment | Signals | Best Next Step |
|---|---|---|
| Hot Buyer | Asked about offers, timing, or second showing | Call or text same day with showing or offer-prep next step. |
| Warm Buyer | Liked home but unclear timing | Send similar homes or buying criteria question. |
| Neighbor Seller Lead | Lives nearby and asks about turnout or price | Offer neighborhood market recap. |
| Future Seller | Mentions possible move later | Offer home value review or prep checklist. |
| Represented Buyer | States they have an agent | Provide public information and suggest they coordinate through their agent. |
| Agent | Attended for client | Professional follow-up about client feedback. |
| Unknown | Little information | Ask one simple question to classify intent. |

## Follow-Up Timing
- Same day: thank-you and one next step.
- Day 1: useful property or market detail.
- Day 3: second-touch question or resource.
- Day 7: close-the-loop or nurture invitation.

## Message Style
Be specific, concise, and connected to the visit. Mention the property, a comment they made, or the next helpful action.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
