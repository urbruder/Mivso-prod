# Inbound Lead Intent Classifier — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Intent Tags
| Tag | Signals | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| Tour-Now Buyer | Showing request, specific property, near-term timing | Call or text immediately with showing options. |
| Financing-Needed Buyer | Payment, pre-approval, or budget questions | Offer lender conversation and buyer consult. |
| Listing-Value Seller | Home value, selling timeline, address submitted | Offer valuation or seller strategy call. |
| Casual Browser | Vague inquiry, no timeline, low detail | Ask one simple question and offer saved search. |
| Neighbor Seller Signal | Open-house neighbor or local homeowner question | Offer neighborhood market recap. |
| Investor | Rent, cap rate, cash flow, portfolio language | Send numbers-focused next step. |
| Unknown | Not enough information | Ask a clarifying question. |

## Urgency Signals
Strong: exact property, showing request, offer question, move deadline, pre-approval, same-day reply. Moderate: saved search, specific area, budget range, recurring views. Low: vague form fill, no reply, no timeline.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
