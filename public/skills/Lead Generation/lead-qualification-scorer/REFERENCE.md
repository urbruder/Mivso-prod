# Lead Qualification Scorer — Reference

## Use This Reference When Running the Skill
Read this file before creating the deliverable. Use it as a compact checklist and language library, not as text to copy word-for-word.

## Default 1–5 Scoring Rubric
| Score | Timeline | Motivation | Financial Readiness | Engagement | Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | Ready now | Clear urgent reason | Pre-approved, cash, or strong equity | Replies quickly | Wants service agent offers |
| 4 | 30–60 days | Clear but not urgent | Mostly verified | Replies within 1–2 days | Good match |
| 3 | 3–6 months | Some reason | Partly known | Occasional replies | Possible match |
| 2 | 6–12 months | Vague | Unknown | Low engagement | Unclear match |
| 1 | No timeline | No stated reason | Not ready or not shared | No response | Poor fit or wrong market |

## Stage Recommendations
- Hot: total score 21–25. Call today and offer a concrete appointment.
- Warm: total score 16–20. Follow up within 48 hours with one useful next step.
- Nurture: total score 11–15. Add to monthly value campaign.
- Watch: total score 6–10. Ask one clarifying question and avoid overinvesting time.
- Low Fit: total score 5 or lower. Close the loop politely or refer if appropriate.

## Risk Flags
Missing contact permission, missing financing, unrealistic price expectations, duplicate record, out-of-area request, unsupported urgency claim, or sensitive personal information that should not be used for scoring.


## Final Quality Check
- The deliverable is a finished visual file, not a raw chat response.
- The client can send, print, or present it without another formatting step.
- The language sounds like a helpful real estate professional, not a generic marketer.
- The file avoids protected-class language, unsupported claims, pressure tactics, and promises of results.
- Every recommended next step is practical, dated where useful, and tied to the lead's situation.
